# Game Overview

## 1. High Concept

**Stillnight Engine** is a retro, Windows 3.1-style RPG that combines dungeon crawling, creature collection, and strategic turn-based combat. It is a single-player experience with a focus on exploration, party management, and resourcefulness.

## 2. Genre

-   **Primary Genre:** RPG (Role-Playing Game)
-   **Sub-Genres:** Dungeon Crawler, Creature Collector, JRPG-style combat.

## 3. Target Audience

-   **Primary:** Players aged 25-40 who grew up with classic 8-bit and 16-bit JRPGs and early PC gaming. They appreciate retro aesthetics, challenging gameplay, and deep systems.
-   **Secondary:** Younger players who enjoy pixel art, indie games, and turn-based combat, and are looking for a unique and challenging experience.

## 4. Unique Selling Points (USPs)

-   **Distinctive Retro Aesthetic:** A faithful and immersive Windows 3.1-inspired visual style that sets it apart from other retro RPGs.
-   **Data-Driven and Moddable:** The engine is built to be highly data-driven, allowing for easy modification and expansion of game content (creatures, items, skills, etc.).
-   **Deep and Strategic Combat:** A turn-based combat system that emphasizes party composition, resource management, and a wide variety of skills and passive abilities.
-   **Modern Vanilla JavaScript Engine:** A lightweight and performant engine built with modern JavaScript standards, without relying on heavy frameworks.

## 5. Game Flow

The player starts with an initial party and is tasked with exploring a multi-floor, procedurally generated dungeon. The core loop consists of:

1.  **Exploration:** Navigating the dungeon, finding items, and encountering enemies.
2.  **Combat:** Engaging in turn-based battles to gain experience and resources.
3.  **Recruitment:** Capturing and recruiting new creatures to add to the party.
4.  **Progression:** Leveling up creatures, learning new skills, and evolving them into more powerful forms.
5.  **Story:** Uncovering the narrative through events and encounters within the dungeon.
